*Objects*

There are two types 

Standard vs Custom Objects in Salesforce

1. STANDARD OBJECTS

Standard Objects are pre-built objects that come with every Salesforce org. They represent common business entities that most companies need, like customers, contacts, and sales opportunities. Salesforce has already designed these with essential fields, relationships, and functionality.

2. CUSTOM OBJECTS

Custom Objects are objects you create yourself to store information that's specific to your business needs. They're like building your own filing cabinet categories when the standard ones don't fit your requirements.

ACCOUNT OBJECT

What it represents:

The Account object represents companies, organizations, or businesses that you have relationships with. Think of it as your corporate address book.

Real-world examples:

* B2B Company: Microsoft, Google, Apple (other businesses you sell to)
* B2C Company: Individual customers can also be accounts
* Partners: Consulting firms you work with
* Vendors: Companies you buy from

 * Key Standard Fields: *

1. Account Name (Microsoft Corporation)

* Industry (Technology)

* Annual Revenue ($198 billion)

* Number of Employees (221,000)

* Phone, Website, Billing Address

* Account Type (Customer, Partner, Competitor)

Business Scenario:

You're a software company. Your Account records include: "ABC Manufacturing" (a customer who buys your software), "XYZ Consulting" (a partner who implements your solutions), and "Tech Solutions Inc" (a competitor you track).

What it represents:
